# Supervisory Guidance on Model Risk Management (SR26-2)

## Definitions

- **model:**
    - A “complex quantitative method, system, or approach that applies statistical, economic, or financial theories to process input data into quantitative estimates.”
    - Includes traditional statistical/quantitative and non-generative, non-agentic AI models.
    - Excludes simple arithmetic, deterministic rule-based processes/software, and generative or agentic AI models.

- **model risk:**
    - “The potential for adverse financial consequences associated with models,” from decisions based on model output.
    - Influenced by inherent risk, exposure, purpose, and use.

    - **inherent risk:**
        - Reflects assumptions, complexity, input quality, and data constraints.
        - Increases with complexity and the criticality/number of assumptions.

    - **model exposure:**
        - Significance of model output to business decisions.
        - Greater for larger portfolios or business impact; often measured quantitatively.

    - **model purpose:**
        - Qualitative consideration of nature and importance of use.
        - Regulatory or financial-risk models are generally higher risk.
        - Together with exposure, determines materiality.

    - **model use:**
        - Depends on understanding limitations.
        - Use beyond intended purpose adds uncertainty and risk.
        - Extensions require analysis of new use, limitations, and controls.

## Guidance on model risk management

- Sets a risk-based approach tailored to model risk profile and size/complexity.
- Practices should be commensurate with risk, materiality, and context.

### Overview

- **Sound practice involves assessing model risk individually and in aggregate.**
    - Aggregate risk reflects model interactions/dependencies, common assumptions/data/methodologies, and simultaneous multi-model effects.

- **Sound model risk management also involves effective challenge.**
    - Critical analysis by objective experts throughout the lifecycle.
    - Requires expertise, independence, and standing/influence to effect change.

- **Size and complexity matter.**
    - Range and rigor depend on size/complexity.
    - Practices may be tailored by model, with rigor commensurate with risk.
    - Model risk should be considered in broader business-risk context.

- **Magnitude of model risk.**
    - Reflects inherent risk in materiality context, determined by exposure and purpose.
    - A sound model can still create high risk if misapplied or misused.

### Model development

- **Alignment with purpose and policy**
    - Development aligns with purpose, business use, and policy.
    - Not purely technical; judgment affects design, inputs, assumptions, methodology, and risk.

- **Clear statement of purpose**
    - Begins with a clear purpose to align with intended use.
    - Supports data, methodology, and testing choices.

- **Input from model users**
    - User input adds business context and practical insight.
    - Managers may question methods/assumptions, especially unexpected outcomes.
    - Constructive engagement improves understanding and quality.

- **Testing during development**
    - Testing evaluates whether the model performs as intended.
    - May include out-of-sample/out-of-time testing, alternatives, and data quality/relevance/input assessment.
    - Rigor is commensurate with complexity and materiality.

### Model use

- Effective use depends on understanding limitations and ongoing performance assessment.
- Use beyond intended purpose adds uncertainty/risk; new use requires analysis of usage, limitations, controls.

### Model validation


- **What validation provides**
    - Reliability insight based on assumptions, methods, data, and relevant theories.
    - Characterizes risk and reveals performance deterioration.
    - Meaningful deviations may warrant adjustment/recalibration/redevelopment.

- **Timing, nature, and frequency**
    - Vary by purpose, methodology, changes, data limitations, and constraints.
    - Generally occurs before first use.
    - Urgent pre-validation use requires attention to limitations, stakeholders, and controls.

- **Quality of validation**
    - Depends on rigor/effectiveness, not merely structure.
    - Requires technical knowledge and modeling expertise.

- **Residual model risk**
    - Material risk can remain.
    - Users should communicate limitations, monitor performance, review relevance, and supplement output.

- **Components of model validation**

    - **conceptual soundness**
        - Assesses/documents design, choices, assumptions, judgments, data selection, construction, and testing.
        - Theoretical construction, interpretability, or benchmarking may apply.

    - **outcomes analysis**
        - Compares outputs with real-world outcomes relative to objectives and business use.
        - May include development testing, monitoring reports, back-testing, outlier analysis.
        - Quantitative analysis evaluates expert judgment.

### Model monitoring

- Evaluates whether a model performs as expected as products, exposures, activities, clients, data, or markets change.
- Deterioration may warrant overlays/adjustment/redevelopment.
- Frequency and scope depend on nature, new data/approaches, and materiality.

### Governance and control

- **General governance principle**
    - Lifecycle components benefit from policies and controls.
    - Policies define expectations and a framework for risk-based practices.
    - Procedures establish monitoring/control and resources.
    - Sophistication reflects model usage and size/complexity.

- **roles and responsibilities**
    - Clear roles, responsibilities, and accountability support MRM.
    - Responsibilities span the lifecycle.
    - Internal audit evaluates rigor, effectiveness, and policy implementation, not duplicating development/validation.
    - External resources require oversight and integration.

- **model inventory**
    - Comprehensive inventory is common practice.
    - Effective inventory supports individual and aggregate risk management.
    - Detail may vary by complexity and model usage.

- **documentation**
    - Supports risk management and continuity.
    - Tracks recommendations, responses, exceptions, remediation.

### Third-party products

- Model risk management principles remain applicable.
- Vendor products should be validated internally or externally.
